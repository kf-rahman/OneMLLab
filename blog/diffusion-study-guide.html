Diffusion Models Learning Plan

By Kazi Rahman March 8,2026

This post is pretty simple. It is my contract and my plan towards building a foundational understanding of diffusion models. My end goal is to build an expertise in world models. Here is how we are breaking down the study plan: 1.The mathematics: - The speciality of Gaussian Distribution - The relationship between Markov Chain and Gaussian Distribution - Wth is reparameterizationt rick? These are some high level mathematical concepts. Its meant to get myself to ask the questions in the right direction and fill in the blanks or review whatever is needed when its needed. Readings: - Blog:** Lilian Weng — "From Autoencoder to Beta-VAE" (builds intuition for latent spaces before diffusion) -Book:** *Deep Learning* by Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville — Ch. 3 (Probability) and Ch. 16 (Structured Probabilistic Models) — free online - **Book:** *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning* by Bishop — Ch. 1–2 for probability grounding So what: Once these questions have been answered along with the readings there needs to be way to really comprehend the information that I graspjed. In the world of LLM coding writing the code line by line is not as useful as being able to make architectural and key decisions that drive the code. So the way i will assess how well I have understood what I learnt is by: - Look at a markov chain that is converging and try to identify its data distribution and how can we modify it -how would we implement KL diveregene between two gaussians - how would we debug a 1d Guassian 2.Score based genrateive models Key concepts: What generative models are trying to do: learn p(x) from data Why likelihood-based models are hard (intractable normalizing constants) Score matching: instead of learning p(x), learn ∇ log p(x) Langevin dynamics: how you sample using the score Noise-conditioned score networks (NCSN) Reading: Paper: Estimation of Non-Normalized Statistical Models by Score Matching — Hyvärinen 2005 (the original score matching paper — read for intuition, not implementation) Paper: Generative Modeling by Estimating Gradients of the Data Distribution — Song & Ermon, NeurIPS 2019 ⭐ (foundational — read this carefully) Blog: Yang Song's own blog post "Generative Modeling by Estimating Gradients of the Data Distribution" — cleaner than the paper Blog: Lilian Weng — "What are Diffusion Models?" — read the first half So what: -What is score matchingg and why would we choose this over maximum likelihood for generative models -How a NCSN works and how can we apply to datasets 3. Diffusion Probabilist Models Key concepts to look at: The forward process: gradually adding Gaussian noise over T steps The closed-form forward process (why you can jump to any noise level directly) The reverse process: learning to denoise step by step The ELBO loss and why it simplifies to a noise prediction objective The U-Net architecture used as the denoiser Variance schedules: linear, cosine Readings: Paper: Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models — Ho et al., NeurIPS 2020 ⭐⭐ (read this 3 times — each time you'll understand something new) Blog: Lilian Weng — "What are Diffusion Models?" — full post Blog: "The Annotated Diffusion Model" — Hugging Face (walks through the paper line by line with code) Video: Yannic Kilcher's DDPM paper walkthrough on YouTube ⭐ Video: Andrej Karpathy — any lecture on generative models for intuition building So what: - Breakdown denoising diffusion probabilistic model -Describe the loss curve for this model 4.Understand the sampling efficiency problem and how it was solved Key concepts: Why DDPM is slow at inference (1000 steps) Non-Markovian processes — breaking the Markov assumption during sampling DDIM: deterministic vs stochastic sampling The trade-off between speed and sample diversity Readings: Paper: Denoising Diffusion Implicit Models — Song et al., ICLR 2021 ⭐ Blog: "DDIM — Denoising Diffusion Implicit Models" — annotated walkthrough by Hugging Face So what: Compare the methodologies 5. Classifier-Free Guidance & Conditional Generation Key concepts: Classifier guidance: using a separate classifier to steer sampling Classifier-free guidance (CFG): conditioning built into the model itself How you condition on text, images, class labels, or any signal Guidance scale — the dial between diversity and fidelity Cross-attention as the conditioning mechanism in U-Nets Readings: Paper: Diffusion Models Beat GANs on Image Synthesis — Dhariwal & Nichol, NeurIPS 2021 (classifier guidance) Paper: Classifier-Free Diffusion Guidance — Ho & Salimans, 2022 ⭐⭐ (short paper, read carefully) Blog: "Classifier-Free Guidance" — Lilian Weng's diffusion series So what:
